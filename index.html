In this study, you will see an image with a blue circle, plus a sentence describing a region in the image. The sentence will refer to an object or location in the image — for example, "the chair on the left side of the room." The sentence will include a phrase "[shown with blue circle]" to indicate which part of the sentence corresponds to what you should be looking for in the blue circle.

Your task is to:

  • Choose "Yes" if any part of the described region is inside the blue circle.
  • Choose "No" if the described object or region is completely outside the blue circle.
  • Choose "Unsure" if you are unsure or the sentence region is ambiguous.

For example, if the sentence describes a chair, and a chair is inside or partially inside the blue circle, choose "Yes".

Some images may have dots or lines on them with numbers or symbols. These should be ignored unless they are part of the sentence description.

Example

Here's an example to help you understand:

Sentence: "I will examine the school bus [shown with blue circle]"

Answer: Yes - because the blue circle overlaps with the school bus

Sentence: "Let's look at the people standing around [shown with blue circle]"

Answer: No - because the people are completely outside the blue circle
